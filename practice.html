Exercise 1: Multiple Choice

Question: Which sentence is grammatically correct?

A) She go to the market every day.

B) She goes to the market every day.

C) She going to the market every day.

D) She gone to the market every day.

✅ Answer: B) She goes to the market every day.

Explanation: In the present simple tense with a third-person singular subject (she), we add "-s" or "-es" to the verb.

Exercise 2: Fill in the Blanks

Question: Complete the sentence with the correct verb form.

I _____ (study) English for two years now.

A) study

B) studies

C) have studied

D) studied

✅ Answer: C) have studied

Explanation: The present perfect tense is used to show an action that started in the past and continues to the present, especially with "for two years."

Exercise 3: Sentence Correction

Question: Identify and correct the error in the following sentence.

"The books on the shelf is very old."

Error: Subject-verb agreement

Explanation: "Books" is plural, so the verb should be "are" not "is."

✅ Correct Sentence: "The books on the shelf are very old."

Exercise 4: Word Order

Question: Arrange the words to make a correct sentence.

coffee / morning / I / every / drink

A) I drink every morning coffee

B) I drink coffee every morning

C) Every morning I coffee drink

D) Coffee I drink every morning

✅ Answer: B) I drink coffee every morning.

Exercise 5: Vocabulary

Question: Choose the word that best completes the sentence.

She gave a _____ speech at the conference.

A) boring

B) eloquent

C) short

D) funny

✅ Answer: B) eloquent

Explanation: "Eloquent" means fluent and persuasive in speaking or writing, which typically describes quality speeches.

Exercise 6: Listening Comprehension

Question: Based on this dialogue, answer the question.

Person A: "Did you finish your project?"

Person B: "Not yet, but I'll complete it by tomorrow."

Question: When will Person B complete the project?

✅ Answer: By tomorrow
