English Grammar Guide

Master the fundamentals and advanced concepts of English grammar

Parts of Speech

Nouns

Words that represent people, places, things, or ideas.

Example: cat, London, happiness

Verbs

Action words or words that describe a state of being.

Example: run, think, is

Adjectives

Words that describe or modify nouns.

Example: beautiful, large, quick

Adverbs

Words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

Example: quickly, very, well

Verb Tenses

Present Simple

Used for general truths, habits, or scheduled events.

Example: I go to school every day.

Present Continuous

Used for actions happening right now.

Example: I am studying English right now.

Past Simple

Used for completed actions in the past.

Example: I went to the market yesterday.

Future Simple

Used for actions that will happen in the future.

Example: I will visit my friend tomorrow.

Sentence Structure

Simple Sentences

Contains one independent clause with a subject and verb.

Example: The cat sleeps.

Compound Sentences

Contains two independent clauses joined by a conjunction.

Example: I like tea, and she likes coffee.

Complex Sentences

Contains an independent clause and one or more dependent clauses.

Example: Although it was raining, we went out.

Common Grammar Mistakes

Subject-Verb Agreement

Incorrect: The team are ready.

Correct: The team is ready.

Pronoun Reference

Incorrect: Each student should bring their book.

Correct: Each student should bring his or her book.

Dangling Modifiers

Incorrect: Running to the store, the rain began to fall.

Correct: As I was running to the store, the rain began to fall.
